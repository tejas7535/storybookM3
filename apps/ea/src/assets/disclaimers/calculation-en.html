General Description on calculation methodology for cradle-to-gate

Schaeffler estimates the Product Carbon Footprint (PCF) related to rolling bearings based on used material and energy resources. In detail, the equivalent CO2 emissions (CO2e) are calculated, which include the effect of carbon emissions as well as other greenhouse gases such as methane or nitrogen oxides, according to their individual contribution to the global warming potential (GWP100). Please be aware that the tool has not been certified in accordance with ISO 14040/44 or any other standard on the calculation of CO2 emissions.

It's important to note that all calculated PCF values are estimates resulting from user input data and assumptions combined with calculation methods and both internal and external data. Therefore, the values calculated by Schaeffler should not be compared with PCF values obtained from other tools or sources unless you are confident about the data sources, methods and assumptions used.

The tool provides estimated CO2e emission values for the bearing production phase.

Emissions related to bearing production phase (cradle-to-gate)

In summary, the PCF values cradle-to-gate are the result of a simplified calculation using an average factor based on the total CO2e emissions of bearing production in relation to the total weight of produced bearings in a reference time period.

The Schaeffler PCF value is calculated by multiplying this average factor with the finished weight of the selected bearing. Due to its simplified top-down calculation approach, it can only provide a first indication of the real PCF of an individual bearing, which may deviate on a case-by-case basis substantially from the calculated average value. Main reasons for this include but are not limited to:

  • the actual steel making process used for the individual bearing
  • the regional energy mix at suppliers' sites producing raw materials / intermediates
  • the regional energy mix at Schaeffler plants producing the finished bearing
  • the heat treatment, machining and final assembly of the individual bearing

The average factor for rolling bearing production determined for the reference year 2021 is set to 3.0 kg CO2e per kg of produced bearing weight. This includes CO2e emissions from Schaeffler operations (GHG Protocol, Scope 1), purchased energy (GHG Protocol, Scope 2), and the purchased goods used for bearing manufacturing including upstream transportation and distribution (GHG Protocol, Scope 3 Upstream).

The PCF is mainly driven by the processing of bearing steel as the predominant material used for rolling bearings. CO2e emissions of other materials used in bearings, such as brass or plastics, are considered to have similar values as steel at first approximation.
