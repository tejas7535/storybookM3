General Description on calculation methodology for production and product use emissions

Schaeffler estimates the Product Carbon Footprint (PCF) related to rolling bearings based on used material and energy resources. The approximated equivalent CO₂ emissions (CO₂e) include the effect of carbon emissions as well as other greenhouse gases such as methane or nitrogen oxides, according to their individual contribution to the global warming potential (GWP100). Please be aware that the tool has not been certified in accordance with ISO 14040/44/67 or any other standard on the calculation of CO₂ emissions.

It's important to note that all calculated PCF values are estimates resulting from input data of the user, implicitly used data of the calculation tool, general assumptions of the calculation methods. Therefore, the values calculated by Schaeffler should not be compared with PCF values obtained from other tools or sources unless you are confident about the data sources, methods and assumptions used.

Changes in data or the calculation methodology may lead to significantly different results. Schaeffler assumes no responsibility for any use of the presented calculations and doesn’t warrant its fitness for any particular purpose.

Schaeffler provides only estimated CO₂e emission values related to rolling bearing production phase (“cradle-to-gate”) and to rolling bearing operation (“product use”) according to the EPD product category rules “Bearings, bearing units and parts thereof (PCR 2023:03)”. Further emissions of other downstream processes (e.g., end-of-life processes) are not included in the calculation. The basic assumptions for a calculation are explained in the following chapters.

For additional information about the climate targets of Schaeffler and our decarbonization strategy, please refer to the latest Schaeffler Sustainability Report.

Emissions related to rolling bearing production phase (“cradle-to-gate”)

In summary, the Schaeffler PCF values “cradle-to-gate” are estimated by multiplying an average factor based on the total CO₂e emissions of rolling bearing production of a reference period and the total weight of a produced rolling bearing.

Due to the simplified top-down calculation approach, it can only provide a first indication of the PCF of an individual rolling bearing, which may deviate significantly on a case-by-case basis from the calculated average value. Main reasons for this include but are not limited to:

  • The actual steel making process used for the individual rolling bearing
  • The regional energy mix at suppliers’ sites producing raw materials / intermediates
  • The regional energy mix at Schaeffler plants producing the finished rolling bearing
  • The heat treatment, machining, and final assembly of the individual rolling bearing

The average factor for rolling bearing production determined for the reference year 2024 is set to 3.9 kg CO₂e per kg of produced rolling bearing weight. This includes CO₂e emissions from Schaeffler operations, purchased energy, and the purchased goods used for rolling bearing manufacturing including upstream transportation and distribution.

Individually calculated PCF values cradle-to-gate currently vary between 1.6 … 7.9 kg CO₂e per kg of produced rolling bearing weight, reflecting above mentioned reasons.

The PCF is mainly driven by processing of rolling bearing steel as the predominant material used for bearings, but also other material categories for bearings, such as brass or plastics, are taken into consideration.

Emissions related to rolling bearing operation (“product use”)

In summary, the Schaeffler PCF values for the “product use” are estimated by multiplying the mechanical drive power to overcome the friction of a rolling bearing, the operating time and the emission factor of the drive energy source.

The frictional power losses are affected by the rolling bearing design, operating conditions and lubrication. To estimate these power losses, the PCF calculation provides results based on the established friction calculation method as implemented in the Schaeffler analysis and design tool Bearinx and our externally provided tools Bearinx-online Easy Friction and Bearinx-online Rotative. Thus, the power losses calculation considers:

  • Internal design of rolling bearings
  • Speed and load of rolling bearings
  • Oil and grease lubrication
  • Rolling and sliding losses
  • Mixed friction and full film lubrication

The emission factors are used as published in the latest VDA report “Emission factors for electricity, district heating, and fuels (updated 09/2024)”. They will be updated with every new release of the report. The emission factors refer to the CO₂e emissions per energy provided by the corresponding energy source. The efficiencies of any energy conversions are neglected.

Please note that the calculated CO₂e emissions refer to the use of the rolling bearing system and can only be used for a first estimation. Under real operating conditions, significant deviations may occur due to unforeseeable circumstances. Further, the following simplifications are made to enable a quick and easy-to-use calculation:

  • Standard operating clearance and mounting conditions
  • Stationary operating conditions
  • No tilting of the shaft and with rigid housings
  • Speed and load are only applied to the inner ring
  • The internal axial force (e.g. for tapered roller bearings) or the preload in the case of arranged bearings, must be compensated with externally applied axial loads in order to be correctly taken into account
  • Very good lubrication and heat dissipation are assumed. This is particularly important for combined loaded cylindrical roller bearings
  • The minimum load for thrust bearings is calculated for the highest speed of all load cases
  • The raceways for cage assemblies are manufactured as specified
